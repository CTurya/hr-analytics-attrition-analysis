# 👥 HR Analytics — Employee Attrition Analysis

**Tools used:** SQL (SQLite) | Looker Studio (coming soon)
**Industry:** Human Resources | People Analytics
**Level:** Entry-level portfolio project

---

## 📌 Business Problem
A company is experiencing higher than average employee attrition
and wants to understand which employees are most at risk of
leaving — and why.

---

## 📂 Dataset
- Source: Kaggle — IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition
- 1,470 employee records
- Fields include: age, department, salary, job role, attrition status

---

## 🔍 SQL Analysis
Four key business questions answered using SQL:
1. What is the overall attrition rate?
2. Which department has the highest attrition?
3. Does salary affect attrition?
4. Which age group is most likely to leave?

---

## 📊 Key Findings

| Finding | Result |
|---|---|
| Overall attrition rate | 16.12% — above 10-15% benchmark |
| Highest attrition department | Sales at 20.63% |
| Highest attrition salary band | Low earners at 28.61% |
| Highest attrition age group | Under 25s at 39.18% |

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
1. Increase base salaries for low income bands — highest single impact
2. Launch a graduate retention programme targeting under 25s
3. Implement a Sales department wellness and incentive programme
